How
Drift
works.
Step 01
Commitments are logged at the moment of disclosure

Every strategic objective is captured from primary sources — prospectuses, earnings calls, investor days, annual reports — at the time of first public statement.

Step 02
Language is tracked across all subsequent disclosures

Each reporting period, we re-read the source material and classify language: Reinforced, Softened, Reframed, or Absent. The delta is the signal.

Step 03
Exits are classified by how they happen

Not all dropped objectives are equal. We classify each exit: Silent Drop, Transparent Retirement, Gradual Phase-out, or Morphed into a successor commitment.

Step 04
The public record is the only source

No insider sources. No proprietary data. No financial model assumptions. Every classification is sourced, dated, and open to challenge. This is accountability, not analysis.

"The most important thing a company can do is say what it means to do — and the most revealing thing it can do is quietly stop saying it."
