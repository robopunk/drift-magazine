01

What Drift is

One paragraph. This is what every piece of communication — every editorial note, every classification, every company page — is ultimately in service of.

The Core Positioning

Drift tracks what companies publicly commit to, how that language changes over time, and what happens to promises that quietly disappear.

For investors, analysts, and strategists who need to know whether a company's stated direction is holding.
All data sourced from public disclosures only. Every classification is evidenced. No proprietary data. No inside sources.
The record is the product.
The Founding Distinction
Most accountability tools measure outcomes. Drift measures the language of commitment — and the silence that follows when commitment fades.
Outcomes are visible to everyone. Language drift is visible only to those paying close attention over time. That is the gap Drift occupies.
What Drift is not
Drift is not a financial analytics tool. It does not model earnings, forecast performance, or rate management quality.
Drift records what was said, when it was said, how the language changed, and what disappeared. The reader draws their own conclusions from the evidence. Drift names the pattern — it does not prosecute the company.
02

Brand personality

Two reference points held in tension. Neither is sufficient alone — the combination is the brand.

The McKinsey side
Precision. Structure. Authority.
Every classification is specific. Every verdict is grounded. Language is tight and unambiguous. The reader trusts that words have been chosen carefully — because they have.
"The objective has been absent from investor communications for three consecutive reporting periods. No exit statement has been issued." Classification language — precise, attributable, conclusive
The journalism side
Independence. Sourcing. Naming.
Drift names what it sees. Not with sensation, not with accusation — but with the willingness to use the right word for the right thing. "Silent drop" is not an accusation. It is a classification.
"Classified: Silent Drop. The original commitment was stated in the prospectus. It has not appeared in any investor communication since Q2 2024." Verdict language — independent, sourced, direct
The tone in one sentence
Drift is the kind of analyst who reads everything, remembers everything, and states conclusions clearly — without ever raising their voice.

Voice — write this, not that

The left column is Drift's voice. The right column contains language that is either too emotional, too vague, or too judgmental for the editorial standard. These are not soft guidelines — they are the line between Drift and opinion journalism.

✓ Write this ✗ Not this
"The objective has been absent from investor communications for three consecutive reporting periods." "The company quietly buried its commitment."
"Language has migrated from volume targets to profitability framing across four disclosure periods." "They changed their story."
"Classified: Silent Drop. Transparency score: Very Low. No exit statement was issued." "They hoped no one would notice."
"The original prospectus stated a site reduction from 26 to 18–20. The current framing contains no numeric reference." "The company walked back its operational promises."
"This is the clearest case of a silent drop in the tracked portfolio." "This is a damning example of corporate evasion."
"The commitment score has declined from 88 to 74 across three consecutive periods." "Confidence in this company is eroding."
"Language classified as Softened: scope reduced, timeline extended, no explanation disclosed." "The goalposts have been moved."
"The record shows no investor communication acknowledging the change in strategic direction." "They said nothing and hoped it would go unnoticed."

04

Objective status classification

Four statuses. Each has a precise definition and a bar for application. Status is assigned editorially — it is not a calculation. When in doubt, apply the more conservative status and monitor.

On Record
Active, evidenced, holding.
The objective has been explicitly referenced in the most recent disclosure of each applicable type. Language is consistent with or stronger than the original statement. No scope reduction, hedging, or timeline extension detected.
"Biosimilar leadership leads the Annual Report narrative for the third consecutive year. 35+ active programmes cited. No language softening detected." Sandoz · Biosimilar Leadership · March 2026
Under Watch
Present, but trajectory shifting.
The objective remains publicly stated but one or more signals indicate a potential change: scope has narrowed, language has become more hedged, or the objective has been absent from one disclosure where it was expected. Not yet drifting — under active monitoring.
"Volume leadership language retained with added caveat: 'targeted expansion in high-return markets.' Minor scope reduction detected." Sandoz · EM Volume Growth · March 2024
Drifting
Measurable language migration detected.
Multiple signals confirm the objective is shifting — either the language has changed substantially across two or more disclosure periods, numeric targets have been removed, or the framing has migrated from the original commitment without explanation. The objective still exists; its shape has changed.
"Three consecutive reporting periods without numeric site targets. Original prospectus commitment not referenced. Current framing: 'efficiency-led manufacturing network.'" Sandoz · Manufacturing Simplification · March 2026
Off Record
Graveyard entry. Commitment has ended.
The objective has been confirmed as no longer active, by any of the four exit manners: silent, phased, morphed, or transparent. Off Record is not a value judgement — a transparently retired objective is as Off Record as a silently dropped one. The difference is in the transparency score.
"Confirmed absent from all investor materials since Q2 2024. No exit statement issued. Classified: Silent Drop. Transparency: Very Low." Sandoz · China Growth Platform · June 2024
Classification rule
Status is changed only when the evidence is unambiguous. One soft signal is not Drifting. One absence is not Off Record. Drift classifies conservatively and monitors actively.
When upgrading a status (e.g. On Record → Under Watch), the editorial note must cite specific language from a specific disclosure. "Language felt weaker" is not evidence. "The term 'volume leadership' appeared in Q4 2023, Q1 2024, and has not appeared since" is evidence.
05

The Momentum Scale

Nine stages from Orbit to Buried. Each is an editorial classification of the current trajectory of a specific objective — how strongly it is being pursued, evidenced, and communicated. The scale maps directly to the timeline: stages above zero sit above the ground line; stages below zero sit below it.

Stage Name Definition Classification criteria
+4OrbitObjective exceeded. Company has redefined upward.Original target met or surpassed; new, more ambitious commitment stated. Evidence of execution confirms overperformance.
+3FlyAhead of schedule. Language reinforced with results.Explicit reaffirmation in most recent disclosure; execution signals (filings, milestones, data) corroborate stated trajectory. Language equal to or stronger than original.
+2RunOn track. Strong momentum, no concerns.Objective mentioned in most recent disclosure; no hedging; no scope reduction; consistent with original language.
+1WalkActive. Progressing steadily.Objective present in disclosures; minor softening acceptable if no pattern; execution signals neutral to positive.
0WatchStanding still. No clear signal either way.Objective mentioned but without progress evidence; or one unexplained absence; or language mildly hedged. Trigger for active monitoring.
−1CrawlSlowing. Language softening detected.At least two signals showing reduced specificity, hedged language, or narrowed scope. Status: Drifting. Timeline or targets may have shifted without explanation.
−2DragSignificant drift. Reframing underway.Framing has changed materially from original commitment. Numeric targets absent for two or more periods. Pattern of deflection in Q&A.
−3SinkEntering graveyard territory. Mostly absent.Multiple consecutive absences across disclosure types. When mentioned, language bears little resemblance to original commitment. Graveyard classification pending.
−4BuriedConfirmed Off Record. Graveyard entry.Objective has not appeared in investor communications for four or more periods across at least two disclosure types. Exit manner classified. Transparency score assigned.
The ground line
Stage 0 (Watch) sits at the ground line. It is the threshold — the point at which an objective is neither clearly alive nor clearly declining. Crossing downward from Watch to Crawl is the editorial event.
The ground crossing is when Drift alerts subscribers and updates the timeline. It is the most important single moment in an objective's lifecycle on the platform — the moment when drift becomes visible.
06

Graveyard exit classification

Not all exits are equal. Drift classifies how an objective ended — because the manner of exit is itself an accountability signal. A company that explicitly retires a commitment is more transparent than one that allows it to silently disappear. The classification is factual, not moral.

⬛ Silent Drop
Disappeared without notice.
The objective was present, then absent — across multiple disclosure types, for multiple periods — with no investor communication acknowledging the change. No explanation. No announcement. The record simply stops.
Detection signal: Four or more consecutive periods of absence across at least two disclosure types, with no retirement statement found in any public document.
⇣ Phased Out
Gradual erosion over time.
The objective did not vanish overnight — it faded. Language weight reduced across three or more periods; prominence declined from headline to footnote to absent. No announcement, but the pattern is visible in the record.
Detection signal: Visible reduction in language prominence across at least three periods, culminating in absence. The decline slope is the evidence.
↝ Morphed
Transformed into a successor commitment.
The original objective was replaced by a related but materially different commitment — often one that retains the spirit of ambition while removing the accountability of specifics. Numeric targets replaced by directional language. Geographic specificity replaced by portfolio framing.
Detection signal: Original language retired; new framing serves a similar narrative function but lacks original specificity. A successor objective is identified and linked.
✓ Transparent Exit
Explicitly retired with explanation.
The company publicly communicated the retirement of the commitment — in a disclosure document, an earnings call, or an investor communication — with an explanation of why the direction has changed. The highest transparency score available.
Detection signal: Explicit statement in a primary source document acknowledging the change in strategic direction or the retirement of a specific commitment.
The transparency score — assigned to every graveyard entry — reflects how visible the exit was to investors and stakeholders at the time it occurred.
ScoreMeaningCriteria
Very Low No communication of any kind. The objective disappeared without any investor communication. No indirect acknowledgment. No framing change. No mention in Q&A. The record simply stops.
Low Minimal or indirect acknowledgment only. A vague reference to changed priorities, or a single indirect comment in Q&A, not in prepared remarks. Not enough to constitute a proper investor communication.
Medium Partial explanation, without full disclosure. A partial acknowledgment — e.g. language change accompanied by a brief rationale, or a passing reference in an investor presentation without a full explanation of the strategic shift.
High Full, explicit communication with rationale. The retirement was explicitly stated in a primary source document, with a clear rationale for the change. Investors had the information they needed to update their models and thesis.

Consistent language across all company pages and classifications.

UI labels — preferred terms All instances, all pages
Sections
Live Commitments — not "Active Objectives"
Off the Record — not "Graveyard" in formal contexts; Graveyard acceptable in UI
The Signal Record — not "Evidence Drawer"
Editorial Assessment — not "Summary"
Commitment Timeline — not "History"
Status labels
On Record — not "Active"
Under Watch — not "Watch" alone
Drifting — not "Declining" or "At Risk"
Off Record — not "Dropped" or "Dead"
Achieved — used sparingly, requires strong evidence
Graveyard exit labels
⬛ Silent Drop · ⇣ Phased Out · ↝ Morphed · ✓ Transparent Exit
These labels appear verbatim on every graveyard entry. Do not paraphrase or vary.

A complete editorial assessment — annotated

The following is an annotated example of a full editorial assessment for a company page. Notes in brackets indicate which brand standard each phrase demonstrates.

Sandoz AG · Editorial Assessment · March 2026 Reference example
Published text
Sandoz enters 2026 with its Golden Decade narrative largely intact — all six active objectives remain publicly referenced. [Precise quantification. "Largely intact" is accurate — not "strong" or "solid."] The biosimilar leadership commitment is the strongest in the tracked portfolio: language has not softened once across nine reporting events. [Evidenced statement. Nine events is a specific claim, not an impression.]

Two objectives show measurable drift. ["Measurable drift" — not "concerning drift" or "worrying signs."] Emerging Markets has migrated from volume leadership to profitability framing without announcement ["Without announcement" is factual. Not "secretly" or "quietly."], and Manufacturing Simplification has lost its specific numeric targets. Three objectives have entered the graveyard — one by silent drop, one by morphing, one by phased retirement. [Classification language used directly. The manner of exit is the story.]
What makes this right
Every claim is verifiable from the signal record. The tone is the same whether the news is good (biosimilar leadership) or bad (China silent drop). No emotional language. No inference beyond what the record shows. The reader is given the classified evidence — the conclusion is theirs to draw.
09

What Drift is building — over time

The brand is not static. As Drift scales from one company to many, the language system accumulates authority. These are the assets that compound.

The classified record
The source of authority
Every signal, sourced and dated, classified and evidenced. The larger the record, the more authoritative the platform. This is why classification rigour is non-negotiable from day one — the record is the product.
The graveyard
The source of differentiation
No other product shows what companies stopped saying, how they stopped saying it, and whether anyone noticed. The graveyard is Drift's most distinctive feature — and the one most likely to be shared, cited, and remembered.
The language patterns
The source of insight
As data accumulates across companies and sectors, patterns emerge: how long before a commitment typically drifts; which disclosure types show the earliest warning; which sectors have the highest silent drop rates. These cross-company insights are the premium product.
The editorial voice
The source of trust
Drift's voice — precise, dry, evidenced, never sensational — is what separates it from opinion and from data. That voice, maintained consistently across every classification and every company page, is what makes the record trustworthy. Voice is infrastructure.
