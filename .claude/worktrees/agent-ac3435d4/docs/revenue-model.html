Path to self-finance
Now — Month 2
Launch with what you have. Publish. Share.
The Sandoz page is good enough to go live. Add 3–4 more companies (VW, BP, Roche — high-interest names). Apply for Carbon Ads. Post every graveyard entry on LinkedIn with a one-paragraph editorial take. The content is the distribution engine.
Revenue target: €50–150/mo (ads only)
Month 3–5
Land the first direct sponsor. Reach 3,000 monthly visitors.
One cold email campaign to 20 pharma/finance data providers. Offer a 30-day sponsored slot at €300 to prove the audience. Build an email list — even 200 subscribers who open your monthly signal digest is enough to show traction.
Revenue target: €300–600/mo
Month 6
Launch the paywall. Sell the first report.
Gate the evidence drawers and graveyard full records behind a €29/mo subscription. Sell the first deep-dive report on whichever company has the most graveyard entries (VW or BP — they write themselves). Stripe in an afternoon.
Revenue target: €800–1,400/mo
Month 12
Self-financing. Time-cost covered. Optionally: first hire.
With 8,000 visitors, 2 sponsors, ~96 subscribers, and 2–3 report sales per month: €1,500–2,500/mo in revenue against €50–90/mo in cash costs. Solidly self-financing. The question at this point is whether to grow or stay small and clean.
Revenue target: €1,500–2,500/mo
Honest Assessment

"PromiseTrack can absolutely self-finance — but not in month three, and not by accident."

The economics are fundamentally sound: niche B2B editorial commands premium ad rates and converts to subscriptions at reasonable rates. The database and agent costs are negligible. The real cost is your time and the patience required for organic search traffic to compound.

The worst-case scenario at realistic assumptions: you spend €600–900 in API and hosting costs over 12 months before breaking even, and you get back a research platform that runs itself and generates €1,500–2,500/mo in year two — on a topic you find professionally valuable anyway. The downside is bounded. The upside, if the content lands with the right audience, is not.
